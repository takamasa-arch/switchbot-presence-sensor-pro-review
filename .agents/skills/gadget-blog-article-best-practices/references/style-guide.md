# DIGILINE Gadget Blog Style Guide

## What to optimize for

Write for readers who already feel a small frustration and want a credible shortcut to a buying decision. The article should feel like a smart friend tested the product and translated the result into ordinary life.

The model article pattern behind this skill is:

- lead with a lived annoyance or doubt
- show the conclusion early
- explain the core technology only as far as it changes daily use
- stay honest about limits, adjustments, and operating cost
- finish with a clear "buy / skip" filter

## Tone model

- Use calm, polished Japanese.
- Keep sentences simple and declarative.
- Prefer "十分実用的だった" over louder praise.
- Prefer "体感として" when reporting impressions rather than measurements.
- Prefer practical wording such as "仕事の流れが止まらない", "置き場所で印象が変わる", "導入後の手間も含めて判断する".
- Avoid marketing language such as "革命的", "神", "圧倒的", "絶対買い".

## Structure prompts

### Title

Use one of these patterns:

- `【実機レビュー】<製品名>は<悩み>を減らせる？<使い方>で試した感想`
- `<製品名>は買いか？<比較軸>から実用目線で判断`
- `<製品名>レビュー: <日常の効果>は本当にあるか`

### Introduction

Make the first 4 paragraphs do this work:

1. name the everyday annoyance
2. state why this product looked relevant
3. narrow the article to one main question
4. promise a practical answer based on actual use

Example pattern:

- `在宅ワーク中、<困りごと>。この小さな不便を減らしたくて、<製品名>を実際に使ってみました。`
- `この記事では、<論点>に絞って、普段どおり使った感想をまとめます。`

### Early conclusion

State:

- who is likely to benefit
- what value was felt most clearly
- what condition or caveat affects satisfaction

Good pattern:

- `最初に結論を書くと、<利用シーン>では満足度が高かったです。`
- `一方で、<設置条件 / コスト / 制約>が合わないと体感差は出にくいです。`

### Feature explanation

Translate technology into daily consequences:

- Bad: `Bluetooth 5.4 / Wi-Fi 6 / mmWave対応です`
- Better: `小さな体動も拾いやすく、長く同じ姿勢でいる場面で差が出ます`

When describing features, always add one of:

- what problem it solves
- what situation exposes the benefit
- what tradeoff it creates

### Main experience section

Prefer subheadings that map to moments of use:

- first setup
- first reaction / startup
- sustained use
- things that required adjustment
- what surprised you after several days

Make each subsection answer:

- what happened
- whether it mattered
- what kind of user would care

### Concerns section

Do not hide the downside. Surface the issue in concrete language:

- battery replacement frequency
- installation sensitivity
- comfort, weight, noise, heat
- ecosystem lock-in
- price versus benefit

When the downside is still uncertain, say so directly:

- `現時点ではここが評価の変動要因です`
- `数週間では断定しにくいので、継続して見たいポイントです`

### Comparison section

Only compare when it sharpens the buying decision. Use compact tables for:

- detection method
- power source
- comfort or maintenance cost
- where the practical difference was felt

Separate official information from personal experience in the surrounding text.

### Buy / skip section

Use simple bullets. These are decision filters, not summaries.

- `買うべき人`
- `見送ってよい人`

Each bullet should describe a situation or priority, not a personality trait.

## Evidence handling

Label evidence quality in the prose itself.

- First-hand use: `実際に使うと`, `私の環境では`, `体感として`
- Official information: `公称では`, `公式情報では`
- Inference: `現時点では`, `可能性がある`, `この前提なら`

If you did not measure a value, do not imply measurement accuracy. Replace false precision with a clear scope note.

## Paragraph and heading rhythm

- Keep paragraphs short, usually 1 to 3 sentences.
- Use H2 for decision-driving themes.
- Use H3 when a section needs moment-by-moment breakdown.
- If two adjacent sections do not change the buying decision, merge them.

## Internal links and CTA

- Link only where another article genuinely helps the decision.
- Introduce the link with a reason, not as a generic "related article".
- End softly. The article should conclude the judgment, not push the click.

## Anti-patterns

- long spec lists before the reader knows why they matter
- generic praise with no scene or comparison
- pretending uncertain battery life, durability, or long-term value is already proven
- overusing rhetorical questions
- writing a conclusion that never tells the reader whether to buy

## Rewrite recipes

### Make the intro sharper

- Find the first paragraph with a real-life inconvenience.
- Move it to the top.
- Delete any earlier company or product overview unless it is essential.

### Make feature paragraphs useful

- Underline each spec mention.
- Add one sentence after it: `つまり、<生活上の変化>`
- Delete the spec if that sentence is weak.

### Make the ending decisive

- Replace vague summary lines with:
  - who gains clear value
  - what tradeoff remains
  - why the recommendation is conditional
